--- layout: post title: Tricky VPN settings on Ubuntu 11.04 date: "2011-05-03T14:03:00.001+02:00" author: Elvis Hsu tags: - Linux - Ubuntu - Tips modified_time: "2014-01-26T13:16:45.915+01:00" thumbnail: /assets/posts/blogger/2011-05-03-image-0000.png blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-1691872865366176972.post-1955202964242078876 blogger_orig_url: https://www.elvishsu.com/2011/05/tricky-vpn-settings-on-ubuntu-1104.html --- Today, I was trying to connect to our VPN with Ubuntu 11.04 and found out that was not working. Well, after a bit searches on Google, I found out there are some points of VPN settings that I needed to change.
  1. Username should be domain\username.
  2. Do not fill out domain in the VPN configuration.
  3. Under the advanced option make sure you tick "Use Point-to-Point encryption (MPPE)" option. (see above screenshot)
